Understanding Search Engine Optimization

What is SEO for ecommerce?

  • SEO is:
  • The process of assessing the current on and off page factors of a website
  • Improving these factors to boost ranking in a search engine like Google
  • Optimization for SEO
  • An act, process, or methodology of making something as fully perfect, functional, or effective as possible
  • Site Traffic affects its search rankings. Building traffic will increase your ranking.

Understanding on-page vs. off-page SEO

  • On-Page Factors:
  • Keywords you’re targeting
  • Written content, headings, titles, etc.
  • Page URLs and the way these are structured
  • internal links
  • Off-Page Factors:
  • Social media, such as Facebook or Twitter
  • Reputable industry sites, ideally from large companies that currently rank well in a similar space
  • Blog posts
  • Product review sites
  • building meaningful relationships with other reputable sites will help increase your ranking.

The importance of usability

  • Usability: making it easy to use and understand for your audience.
  • Acquisition (SEO) and Retention (Usability) are the same thing. The longer a user stays on the site the better SEO will become.
  • Click-Throughs: when a user explores deeper into the site.
  • Design and write for customers first, then search engines
  • Never sacrifice usability for rankings
  • Give visitors direct access to information-don’t bury it.
  • focus on proper design techniques

Exploring how SEO affects the buying process

  • Post Purchase Evaluation:
  • Need Recognition: think about a problem they need solve
  • Solution Explored: seek out a solution for the problem
  • Evaluate Options: explore options to solve the problem
  • Buy!: select the product and purchase
  • Evals
  • Shifting your SEO up higher in the need framework you can target users who have a problem but haven’t settled on a solution.
  • Shifting your SEO efforts lower can allow you to out compete your competitors on customers who are already interested.

Setting expectations and establishing realistic goals for your SEO results

  • Target a niche, better to be a big fish in a small pond
  • Try to make more sales from a targeted group of buyers
  • Just because your site is better than one of your competitors doesn’t mean you will have a higher SEO score if they have off-page links.
  • Fake it till you make it. Just give Google money to get to the top.

Research Your Customers

Understanding exactly who your customer is

  • Confirm who your customers are.
  • what demographics do they belong to?
  • Where do they live?
  • What are their other buying patterns online?
  • What is their social Security #? Can they be blackmailed? They have money and we want it! Coffee is for closers! *not actual notes. actual notes started to get creepy.
  • You can survey previous customers to get an idea about who they are.
  • Identify themes in customers that can be applied to keywords.
  • Group customers into buyer “personas”
  • Target customers who are more likely to purchase, instead of just visiting.

Creating buyer personas

  • The goal is to create a person or identity that exactly matches the type of buyer your site is going to attract.
  • This will help better tailor your messaging and serves as a reference point
  • Get an idea who your customers are based on data.
  • Create a person(s) based on that data. Give them an age, job title, income, hobbies, spending habits.
  • Buying personas can help you gain Quality traffic

Using simple and long tail keyword searches

  • Keyword research is probably the most important aspect of your Ecommerce SEO strategy.
  • The sites at the top of the search results gets the most hits and sales.
  • Search volume drops, the higher the number of search terms are added.
  • Simple Keywords
  • 1-2 words targeting a broad product or service
  • High competition, high search volume
  • Expensive and difficult to target
  • Long-Tail Keywords
  • 3+ words
  • Low competition, low search volume
  • More focused customer, may be more likely to buy

Getting the most out of keyword research

  • write out an initial list of keywords that a buy may enter
  • Use Google’s suggestion feature to get an insight into what people are searching for
  • search for a keyword planner, fill out the form with information regarding your service and your target audience.
  • Then use the results to look at keyword rankings for ideas.
  • Explore competitors for keyword ideas.
  • Make sure your site contains both short and long-tail keywords

Carrying out competitor research

  • Perform a search for each keyword and make note of any major players in that field.
  • Use SEMrush to analyze other high ranking sites for that particular keyword.
  • Run a side by side SEO compairisons (internet marketing ninjas)
  • differentiate yourself and focus on a niche market.

Auditing for Site Usability and Structure

Harnessing  page titles and meta information

  • The Page title is one of the most important elements when it comes to keywords
  • Page Titles:
  • 50-60 characters max
  • use important keywords close to the beginning
  • use a unique page title for each page of the site.
  • Description:
  • useful for helping the users know what a page is about
  • 150-160 characters
  • Written in a clear, easy to understand way
  • Use a unique description for each page on the site.

Exploring page structure: Headings, copy, and images

  • Headings should be prominent and semantic
  • Use heading to help users understand what your page is about
  • product descriptions should use relevant keywords.
  • The Image alt tag can be used to help boost your SEO by making use of keywords to describe what the image is of.

Crafting internal links to help search engines explore your site

  • Well crafted links help users navigate the site
  • footers that contain helpful links to navigate can assist users in exploring your site search engines in crawling through all your pages.
  • site links
  • avoid using generic anchor text like “Click Here”
  • Do not use images as links as there is no anchor text present. If you must use an image, be sure to include alt text.
  • Large footer areas are a great location for internal links
  • Persistent Navigation: internal links are always visible.
  • unseen pages won’t be ranked

Earning inbound links to raise your ranking

  • External link: any link from an outside site that points to your site
  • The better the reputation of the site pointing to yours, the more valuable the link is towards your SEO.
  • If possible, have third-party sites link to your site using relevant anchor text.
  • Sites with a google pagerank of 5 or more is very valuable.
